Built for the 90% use case

Sometimes you just need something simple.

You don't need a 3D visualisation. You're not an interior designer. You already have your floor plan — maybe it's a PDF from your estate agent, a photo you took, or just a rough sketch. You just need to know: will this sofa actually fit? What will the whole thing cost? Can I send this to my partner so we're on the same page before we start buying?

Professional tools like SketchUp or AutoCAD are powerful but take days to learn. Apps like IKEA's planner are locked to one brand. Generic drawing tools don't understand furniture or scale.

Plan/Scale is the gap between those extremes — a focused tool that does one thing well. Upload your floor plan image, use the built-in two-point calibration tool to set real-world scale in seconds, draw your rooms, browse a catalogue of real furniture filtered to your region, drag pieces into position at their actual dimensions, and see your running cost total update as you go. When you're done, export a room-by-room PDF or copy a share link. That's it.

How it works

Six steps from blank canvas
to checkout.

01
⌖
screenshots/step-01-calibrate.png

Upload your floor plan

Drop in any image of your floor plan — a PDF scan, estate agent drawing, or phone photo. Use the two-point calibration tool to click a known distance on screen (e.g. a wall you know is 4m) and the app sets the real-world scale instantly.

02
⬡
screenshots/step-02-draw-rooms.png

Draw your rooms

Click and drag directly on the canvas to draw each room at real-world size. Rooms can be any shape — drag corner handles to create L-shapes, bay windows, or alcoves. Each room shows its name and live area in m².

03
⊞
screenshots/step-03-catalogue.png

Browse & place furniture

Open the furniture catalogue to browse real products from IKEA, Muuto, HAY, West Elm, BoConcept, and more — filtered by your region and price range. Add items to your plan and drag them into position at their exact physical dimensions. Don't see what you need? Type in any custom piece by name and size — a sofa you already own, a built-in unit, anything.

⚠ Note on dimensions Catalogue sizes are sourced from retailer data and are a close guide, not a guarantee. Always confirm exact dimensions on the product page before purchasing. Once added to your plan, you can edit any item's size manually to match the spec sheet.

04
⎘
screenshots/step-04-share.png

Save or share your plan

Your plan saves automatically to your browser as you work — refresh the page and it's still there. For longer-term storage, download a .json project file. Or hit Share to generate a link that encodes your entire plan in the URL — send it to anyone and they can open it instantly, no account needed.

05
⎙
screenshots/step-05-pdf.png

Export a PDF

Export your plan as a PDF — the full floor plan, or one focused page per room with its furniture list and cost breakdown. A ready-made reference for when you head to the shops or share it with a partner or flatmate.

06
↗
screenshots/step-06-buy.png

Click through and buy

Every catalogue item links directly to the product page on the retailer's website. Click through, add to cart, and buy — knowing it fits, knowing the total, and knowing you're done in one session.
